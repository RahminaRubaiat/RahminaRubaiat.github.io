About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the Knight Foundation School of Computing and Information Sciences, Florida International University. My research focuses on artificial intelligence, speech processing, and digital health, with an emphasis on explainable and trustworthy machine learning systems for neurological disorders and brain injuries.

I develop deep learning models and digital biomarkers from speech and related modalities to support early detection and monitoring of concussion and neurodegenerative diseases. I am particularly interested in human-centered, equitable AI for healthcare, and in building tools that are clinically meaningful, interpretable, and deployable in real-world settings.

Research

My current research centers on speech-based deep learning for concussion detection and recovery tracking. By transforming raw audio into representations such as mel spectrograms and MFCCs, I build and analyze convolutional and hybrid neural architectures to distinguish concussed individuals from healthy controls.

A key aspect of my work is explainability: I integrate SHAP and related interpretability methods to identify which acoustic and temporal features best capture neurological impairment. This work contributes to clinically trustworthy AI systems that can assist clinicians while remaining transparent and accountable.

Longer-term, I aim to establish an AI and Digital Biomarker lab focused on multimodal data fusion (speech, sensor data, facial cues, cognitive responses), responsible and fair AI, and mobile/telehealth deployment for neurological and mental health applications.
